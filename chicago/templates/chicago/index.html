Engaging Chicago City Council

Chicago City Council is the legislative body of the City of Chicago. It consists of 50 elected aldermen from 50 wards. City Council meets monthly and is presided over by Rahm Emanuel, the Mayor of Chicago. The secretary is Susana Mendoza, City Clerk of Chicago.

Chicago Councilmatic tracks all things related to Chicago City Council: the legislation introduced and passed, it's various committees and the meetings they hold, and the aldermen themselves. Read more about how City Council works »

Citywide issues

The Mayor of Chicago generally sets citywide policy. This includes business transactions, changes to the municipal code, issuing bonds and appointments.

View citywide legislation

Local issues

At the local level, Aldermen have control over all matters in their ward, including business permits, land use, parking and residents issues.
