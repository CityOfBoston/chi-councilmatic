NYC Councilmatic is free, non-profit, and non-partisan - the first website of its kind for open access to official NYC city council information.



How does New York City Council work, anyway?

The New York City Council has 51 council seats, with each representative typically serving a four year term.

The Council is an equal partner with the Mayor in governing New York City. Its responsibilities include:

  • Discussing and approving local legislation
  • Making land use decisions
  • Approving the city's budget

Introductions are the most interesting type of legislation because they are proposals for new laws. Once an introduction is passed, it becomes a Local Law - see all Local Laws for 2015.

Most of the Council's legislative action happens in committees. Each committee meets in public hearings 2-3 per month - there, proposed legislation is debated. Members of other government branches, as well as the public, are able to comment at these hearings.

Meetings of the entire Council, referred to as Stated Meetings, occur twice a month at City Hall.



Open Data, Open Source

NYC Councilmatic is built on top of Open Civic Data, a project that standardizes information about people, organizations, events and bills at multiple levels of government.

This web app is open source and on GitHub - meaning that anyone can re-use or adapt the code.

If you're interested in bringing Councilmatic to your city, we'd love to hear from you!



Team

NYC Councilmatic is brought to you by the Participatory Politics Foundation, a NYC-based 501(c)3 non-profit organisation with a mission to increase civic engagement. David Moore is the program manager, based out of the Civic Hall community space.

The site was built by DataMade, a Chicago-based civic technology company specializing in data visualization and exploratory web apps.
